Quick reference

Why Unit Conversion Errors Happen

Unit conversion mistakes are a common source of real-world errors — from cooking disasters to, famously, NASA's 1999 Mars Climate Orbiter loss caused by a mix-up between metric and imperial measurements in spacecraft navigation calculations. Most countries use the metric system, while the US primarily uses imperial units, making conversion a frequent practical need for travel, cooking, construction, and science.

This tool covers the five most commonly needed conversion categories, using precise conversion factors rather than rounded approximations, to minimize compounding errors in multi-step calculations.

Key Conversion Factors

1 meter = 3.28084 feet
1 kilogram = 2.20462 pounds
1 mile = 1.60934 kilometers
°F = (°C × 9/5) + 32

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do my manual conversions sometimes differ slightly from this tool? ▼
Many commonly memorized conversion factors are rounded for convenience (like using 1.6 instead of 1.60934 for miles to km), which can introduce small errors that compound over multiple steps or large quantities. This tool uses precise, unrounded conversion factors throughout.
Why is temperature conversion different from the others? ▼
Unlike length, weight, or speed (which use simple multiplication), temperature scales have different zero points — 0°C is not the same physical temperature as 0°F. This requires an offset-based formula rather than a simple multiplication factor, which is why temperature conversion looks different mathematically.
Which countries still use imperial units? ▼
The United States is the largest country still primarily using imperial units (feet, pounds, Fahrenheit) for everyday measurement, alongside Liberia and Myanmar to varying degrees. Nearly all other countries have adopted the metric system as their official standard.
